From underground inception...

...to global traction!

Electronic dance music, also known as EDM, is known for its repetitive beats that drive the pulse of the music and at most times, layer on additional tracks to create an immersive experience. With its wide variety of genres and subgenres such as house, trance, techno, dubstep, EDM pop, chillout and more, EDM encapsulates an endless number of creative sounds and techniques that distinguish the genres from each other. Nevertheless, there is one characteristic that all EDM songs have in common: the drop, a feature that creates an intense pleasure among listeners.

Due to differences in musical interest and perception, the drop may evoke unique emotions. Those who typically find joy in listening to EDM may experience the benefits of higher well-being and concentration when using it as background music (BGM) for studying. Although there is often a stigma that EDM tracks are just heavy and loud sounds thrown together, the music is more than that. Really, it is the perception of its complex sounds that drive the ability to appreciate it as BGM.

Not only does sound perception matter, but so does the influence of online culture on EDM. The trends and conversations about EDM on TikTok and other social media platforms reflect the impression that people have about the genre as well as influence rave culture and what DJs and producers choose to add into their music.

With that being said, this website explores the following:

♬ How does the drop in EDM contribute to its experience and promote well-being and focus?
🗒 In what ways can EDM be used as study BGM?
🔗 Lastly, how do TikTok trends and conversations surrounding EDM influence its music?
