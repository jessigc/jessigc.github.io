How Internet Trends Shape EDM


Exploring #TikTok

While the emergence of EDM dates back to the early 1970s, it started to become a global phenomenon in the early 2010s, simultaneously with the birth and popularization of social media and Internet culture. Naturally, the Internet became a place for DJs to promote new music and currently continues to reinforce conversations surrounding the EDM culture and soundscape. According to Spencer 2022, ‚Äúthe Internet changes what a music genre is in the twenty-first century.‚Äù The perception that people have about EDM is influenced by the content they see online, while the trends seen on social media reflect what the culture is. Simply put, online culture leaves a lasting impact on offline culture and offline culture leaves a lasting impact on online culture, in a coinciding manner.

When producing tracks, DJs take notes of the type of sounds and trends that the EDM community gears towards and integrate them into their personal style, which is especially true of drops in the dubstep and bass realm. The ‚Äúbass face‚Äù term originated on the Internet and is an expression ‚Äútriggered by the precise onset of the drop,‚Äù as listeners always expect the drop to eventually occur. This expression is a ‚Äúweb-based depiction‚Äù that reinforces itself through public values about EDM communicated online and offline. Namely, posts and memes related to bass face set expectations of what a drop should feel and sound like, therefore influencing EDM styles (Spencer 2022).

According to Spencer (2022), many listeners experience being taken aback when artists play a familiar build-up, cut it, and drop it into another track. This illustrates that artists recognize a lot of what people enjoy based on TikTok and Internet trends.

Popular Bass DJ, Excision, describes that the intensity of the music has a big impact on its listeners. He understands that with dubstep, there is a lot of emotion and rage that can be released on the dance floor. This is essentially EDM culture and it all comes back to the concept of the drop. The drop has value because of how it is advertised online. Artists often post short-formed videos on social media platforms that depict a drop or two along with a clip of the crowd going crazy. This sells the experience and again, creates expectations for EDM tracks and culture that DJs align with. Essentially, the reaction to the drop and the music overall is what both influences and becomes the brand (Spencer 2022). But one of the most important and beautiful parts is that although they are influenced by online culture, artists always stay true to their own unique sounds and passion.
